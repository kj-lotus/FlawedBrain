**The Hunt** (Screening and Selection)

Week 1-4

In this phase the main objective is to find a publicly traded company that is fundamentally sound but operationally distressed. This allows us to implement our fix to bring up shareholder values.

How we will screen: 
The group will use Finviz or Capital IQ or other research platforms in order to screen for "Laggard Profile"
Sector: Retail, Industrial, or Consumer Goods (Must have physical inventory).

Inventory Turnover: Bottom 25% of the industry.

Gross Margin: Lower than peer average (indicates high logistics costs or markdowns).

Stock Performance: Flat or underperforming (meaning the market has priced in the inefficiency).


In order to analyze this company we need something to compare it to.
We need a target and a Gold standard.

For example we have Gap, a "fat" company and we need to compare it to Zara a "lean" company. 

Having a company we can compare to leads us to the final few steps

Dupont Analysis: Break down Return on Equity (ROE). Is the company failing because of low margins or low asset turnover? We want to find companies with Low Asset Turnover.

The "Sniff Test": Read the CEO's latest earnings call transcript. Are they complaining about "excess inventory" or "supply chain headwinds"? If yes, that’s our target.

Deliverable:
A side-by-side comparison table of the Target vs. Competitor with the thesis: "Company X is undervalued because it holds $500M in excess inventory compared to peers. Releasing this cash is the catalyst for a stock rally."
