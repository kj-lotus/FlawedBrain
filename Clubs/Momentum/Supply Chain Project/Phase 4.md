**The Visualization**

Week 12-14

This phase is about showing the financial impact of the improvements we pitched in phase 3.

We take the company's financial sheets and input our "Bull case" assumptions.

Balance Sheet: Inventory decreases by $200M. Cash increases by $200M (This is the one-time release of working capital).

Income Statement: COGS decreases (due to better vendor terms/less storage cost). EBITDA margin expands.

The DCF (Discounted Cash Flow) Model:

Free Cash Flow (FCF) Boost: Since we are reducing Net Working Capital, that reduction acts as a positive addition to Free Cash Flow.

The Multiplier Effect: Higher FCF leads to a Higher Terminal Value, which leads to a Higher Enterprise Value.

The Final Pitch Narrative:

"We issue a BUY rating on *Company* with a target price of $XX (20% upside). While the market sees a declining retailer, we see a Working Capital turnaround play. By implementing our proposed SKU rationalization and logistics fixes, the company can unlock $200M in trapped cash, driving FCF yield from 4% to 7%."

Deliverable:
A professional Investment Committee Memo (4-5 pages) and a Final Presentation Deck.
