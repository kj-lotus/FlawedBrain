Comparative Advantage vs Alternative
	how do we find if we have comparative / competitive advantage
		- If you can produce things cheaper or faster. tighter quality control. better materials
		- tailored towards specific customer, makes price make sense for them

Create Value - Increases advantage
	- increase willingness to pay
		- people willing to pay for a service or product that solves a problem
		- different for different people and also different for the same person at different times
	- lower cost
		- cost: cost to the company NOT price (what customer pays)
		- cost to produce
			- traced all the way back through value chain
		- having high cost means that prices need to increase (revenue) and if that is set above willingness to pay then you can lose customers
	- lowering cost is good as long as it doesn't hurt willingness to pay
	- create new markets
		- 
Capture Value
	- leverage bargaining power/market position
		- getting the surplus feels good as a consumer
		- at each point of supply chain want to add value
			- adds producer and consumer surplus
	- leverage resources, relationships and other assets

How to Compete Successfully
	- until someone buys it - willingness to pay is a theory
	-the theory can be based on anything
		- people will pay for a lot of different things because they have different needs and desires
	- effective strategy
		- integrate an understanding of what is the external environment
			- know the industry
			- adjacent industry
			- competitors
		- match internal resources to do better than competitors and substitutes
	- economic logic of how firms create and capture value

Not Strategy
	- if it cannot actually lowering costs or willingness to pay
		- marketing campaign
		- pricing changes
		- replacing old equipment
	- operational effectiveness
		- having efficient processes
		- good morale / team building
	- an easily reversible action
		- competitor can also do this
			- instead use resources to do something new that competitors cannot yet
