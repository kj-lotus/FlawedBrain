	Relevant revenues and costs
		Differential Revenues
		Differential costs
			ex. higher grade leather for a football is going to cost more money than before
			$5 for football on tues then $7 for footballs on thursday for higher grade leather - $2 increase in cost
			$15 price for football tues then $18 on thurs - $3 increase in revenue
		Opportunity costs
			Choose one course of action over another
			ex. Spring break - work and get $500 or take a vacation to florida
				oportunity cost of going to florida is $500 - you gave up making that money to go to florida.
	Irrelevant costs
		Sunk cost
			Ignore sunk costs
				invest a ton of money and want to be the first to have a new product or manufacturing technique
				someone beats them to it
				they spent millions on facilities and working on this product or innovation
				can't really ignore all this money that you used on manufacturing plants

Review Problems 1
	1. The tables are made of wood that costs $100 per table
		- Product Cost
			- Variable - more tables being made, more wood needed
			- Direct Materials
	2. Assembly of tables - wage cost $40 per table
		- Product cost
			- Variable cost - more tables more workers to be paid
			- Direct Labor
	3. factory supervisor paid 45k a year
		- Product cost 
		- Fixed Cost - salary
		- Manufacturing Overhead - indirect costs of running a factory
	4. Electrical costs $2 per machine hour and 4 are being used to build tables
		- Variable Cost
		- Manufacturing Overhead
	5. Depreciation on machines used to make tables
		- Period Cost
		- Fixed Cost - straight-line depreciation (10k depreciation per year)
		- Manufacturing Overhead
		- Sunk Cost
	6. Salary of President is $200k per year
		- Period Cost
		- Fixed Cost
	7. Company spends $250k a year on advertising
		- Period Cost
		- Fixed Cost
	8. Sales person paid $30 per table
		- Period Cost
		- Variable Cost
	9. Instead of producing tables, Company rents a factory space for 50k a year
		- "Instead of" give away for opportunity cost
		- Opportunity Cost
