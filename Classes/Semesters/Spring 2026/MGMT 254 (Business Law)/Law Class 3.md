Equity - what is just, fair, and right
	Quasi Contract
		Exception to a legally enforceable contract
			You ship goods to the wrong company by accident. They use them and make money. Court makes them pay what the goods are worth. No real contract needed

Ch 2
	Plaintiff
		person or business who starts a lawsuit
			claim other side did wrong and ask court for help
	Defendant
		Person or business being sued
			accused of keeping a benefit without paying for it
	Opening statements
		Basic story to judge and jury without argue
			Plaintiff first
				talks about why defendant is wrong and explain why
			Defendent second
				give their side of the story
	Voir Dire
		Lawyers and judge question potential Jurors before trial starts
			Mainly to figure out any biases 
		Good Voir Dire ensures that the Jury listens to facts only
	Preemptive Challenge
		Allows lawyers to kick out any jury members no reason necessary
			mainly used if bias is suspected amongst any jury members
				allows for good voir dire
	Summons & Complaint
		Summons - court notice telling defendant they are being sued
			they must respond or loose by default
		Complaint - paper from plaintiff listing facts, claims and what they want from defendant
	Answer
		defendants written response  to the complaint.
			admit or denies each claim and lists their defenses
	Counterclaim
		defendant sues plaintiff back in same case
	Cross - complaint
		one defendant sues a different defendant or co-party
	Discovery - Deposition & Interrogatories
		both sides swap facts and proof before trial. 
			It helps everyone know the full story so no surprises happen in court.
		Deposition
			mini interview under oath
				lawyer asks questions in room with witnesses and every word is recorded
		Interrogatories
			written question sent to other party
				must answer in writing under oath
					"list all witnesses", "describe accident"
	Motion for Summary Judgment
		request to judge to decide case without full trial
			After discovery, one side files it saying "No real fight over facts—judge rule now."
	Judgment Notwithstanding the Verdict
		Judge overrules jury decision after trial
			Judge steps in if the jury's call makes no sense based on the proof shown.
	Garnishment
		court order that grabs owed money directly from loser's paycheck, bank account or other holdings
			IF loser cannot pay the owed money
			maximum amount that court can take from salary / wage
	Mediation
		neutral person helps both sides talk and settle on an agreement without trial
			Parties meet, often after filing suit. Mediator explains rules, each side shares their story, then private talks happen. No judge decides—everyone must agree.
	Arbitration
		private mini trial where neutral expert (arbitrator) hears both sides and makes a final binding decision instead of going to court
			Parties agree upfront (often in contracts) to use it. They pick the arbitrator, share evidence, have a hearing, then get a ruling that's hard to appeal. Faster and private, but no jury.
		Required in Insurance
	Moot
		Issue doesn't matter anymore
			Court throws out the case if nothing can fix the problem now. No winner needed since the fight ended on its own.
