Civil vs Criminal Law
	- Criminal law deals with punishing the defendant 
		- Burden of Proof: amount of proof the jury has to hold to prosecute them
			- "Beyond Reasonable Doubt"
				- The evidence must be so compelling that jurors have no logical reason to doubt the defendant's guilt; it's not about eliminating all possible doubt, but reasonable doubt.
	- Civil law deals with fraud and damages
		- Burden of Proof: Preponderance of the Evidence
			- Evidence shows that something is more likely true than it isn't
			- Think of a scale; if the plaintiff's evidence weighs even slightly more (51%) than the defendant's, they've met this burden.
