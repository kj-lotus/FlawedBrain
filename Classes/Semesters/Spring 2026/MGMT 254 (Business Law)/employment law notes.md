## I. Civil Rights Act (Title VII, 1964)

## Historical Context

The Civil Rights Act traces back to JFK's **executive order** barring discrimination in federal contractor businesses based on race, religion, color, and national origin. After JFK's assassination, President LBJ pushed Congress to pass Title VII in **1964**. The word **"sex"** was actually added by a House Representative _trying to kill the bill_ — it passed anyway, leaving little legislative history defining what "sex" means. Courts have since interpreted "sex" as relating to gender.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## Who Is Covered

- Organizations with **15 or more employees** must comply[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Covers: **hiring, firing, promotion, transfer, compensation, and admission to training programs**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- The five protected classes: **Race, Color, Religion, Sex, National Origin**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    

## Why These Five Classes?

JFK's personal history drove three of the five:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)

- **Race/Color** — The primary political driver; African Americans faced widespread workplace discrimination[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Religion** — JFK was the first (and still only) Catholic president and personally experienced religious discrimination growing up in a Protestant environment[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **National Origin** — JFK's ancestors were Irish immigrants; Irish immigrants faced significant discrimination following the potato famine of the 1850s[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Sex** — Added almost accidentally, but courts have defined and expanded it[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## II. Bona Fide Occupational Qualification (BFOQ)

A **BFOQ** is a legal exception allowing employers to discriminate based on **sex, religion, or national origin** when genuinely required for the job. **Race and color can NEVER be a BFOQ** — this is an absolute rule.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## Religion BFOQ

- A Catholic church can hire a priest over a more-qualified rabbi or pastor when the role involves promulgating religious doctrine[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- This does **not** apply to clerical/administrative positions — only roles directly tied to spreading the religion's message[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **TWA v. Hardison (Supreme Court)**: An employer only needs to make **reasonable accommodation** for religious practices — paying time-and-a-half to cover a worker's Sabbath was ruled an _unreasonable_ burden[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- A "deeply held religious belief" does **not** require membership in an organized religion[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Otis Elevator case**: A woman refused to wear pants for religious reasons. Employer required pants for safety. She proved form-fitting women's clothing was equally safe, removing the safety justification → employer required to reasonably accommodate[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Piercings case**: Employee claimed religious belief required her to keep piercings visible. Employer offered to let her cover them. She refused → termination was **legal** because employer offered reasonable accommodation[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    

## Sex BFOQ

- **Stereotyping is prohibited**: An employer can't say "women can't lift 40+ lbs" — too many women can[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Modeling and acting **are** BFOQs for sex:
    
    - A sitcom can require a woman to play a mother's role[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
        
    - Victoria's Secret can require female models for a lingerie runway[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
        
    - **Hooters**: Argued servers' primary role is "modeling," not food service, to justify hiring only women (EEOC sued for $22 million)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
        
- **Pan Am Airlines (Supreme Court)**: Customer preference for female flight attendants is **not** a valid sex BFOQ — courts won't allow stereotyping to justify discrimination[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Men's tailoring shop**: Courts upheld a BFOQ for male-only tailors in a men's clothing store based on **modesty** during inseam fittings[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    

## Race/Color — Absolute Protection

- There is **no BFOQ** for race or color under any circumstances[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Cosby Show example**: Even if a character is written as Black, you cannot legally exclude a white actor from auditioning — no BFOQ for race[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **The Pelican Brief example**: If Denzel Washington were denied a role based on race/color, he could sue for damages including **punitive damages** with **no cap**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/1be07146-aeb3-4f2b-a53a-10e07f1c62d2/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt1-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    

## Damages Cap by Protected Class

|Class|Damages Cap|
|---|---|
|Sex, Religion, National Origin (≤100 employees)|$50,000 max|
|Sex, Religion, National Origin (larger companies)|Increases with size|
|**Race / Color**|**No cap — punitive damages available**|

[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## III. Legal vs. Illegal Discrimination

Many forms of discrimination are **legal** because they don't fall under the Civil Rights Act's protected classes:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

- **Sexual preference/orientation**: No federal protection; Indiana has **no state protection** either[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
    - A city like Indianapolis may have a local ordinance providing protection[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
        
    - Purdue's internal policy prohibits it for employees — but **not** for applicants before hiring[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
        
- **Accents**: A Southern accent alone is not a protected class — a CA company can reject a Harvard MBA candidate for having a slow Southern accent for a fast-paced sales role[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
    - **BUT**: If the accent is tied to national origin (e.g., Spanish, Indian, Chinese dialect), it becomes potential illegal discrimination[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
        
- **Tattoos**: Non-religious tattoos are legal to discriminate against; religious tattoos trigger the reasonable accommodation obligation[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- **Smokers**: No federal law protects smokers from hiring discrimination; self-insured companies can legally refuse to hire them[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- **Intelligence/common sense**: Legal to discriminate[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- **Professional credentials**: Legal to require law degrees, medical licenses, etc.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    

## International Civil Rights Act Application (1991 Amendment)

Prior to 1991, U.S. companies abroad had to follow the Civil Rights Act everywhere. After the amendment, three exceptions apply:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

1. **Foreign law exception**: If a foreign country's laws conflict with the Civil Rights Act, the **foreign country's laws prevail** (e.g., a woman can't drive in Saudi Arabia → legal to not hire her as a driver there)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
2. **Treaty exception**: Treaties rank above statutes in the hierarchy (Constitution → Treaties → Statutes). A U.S.-Japan treaty allows Japanese companies to favor their own citizens over Americans in the U.S.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
3. **Work authorization exception**: An employer can legally decline to hire someone who lacks legal authorization to work in the U.S. — this is **not** national origin discrimination[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
    - Employers **cannot** ask "Are you a U.S. citizen?" — but **can** ask "Do you have legal authorization to work in the U.S.?"[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## IV. Sexual Harassment

Sexual harassment is treated as a form of **sex discrimination** under the Civil Rights Act. It is **not** limited to opposite-sex situations — the Supreme Court confirmed men can harass men, women can harass women.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## What Constitutes Sexual Harassment

- Verbal: Comments about body, sex life, slang terms (e.g., calling someone the slang term for "cat"), pet names like "honey/sweetheart" (in the workplace, if unwelcome)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- Physical: Unwelcome hugging, fondling, assault, rape[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- Visual: Sexually suggestive posters, calendars, cartoons, pictures — **only for employees**, not customers or visitors[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
- Gestures[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    

## Two Types of Sexual Harassment

|Feature|Quid Pro Quo|Hostile Work Environment|
|---|---|---|
|**Meaning**|"Something for something"|Severe/pervasive harassment creating hostile environment|
|**Who can harass**|Supervisor → Subordinate **only**|Supervisors, co-workers, even customers|
|**Employer defense**|**None** — always liable|**Yes** — if they investigated and acted reasonably|
|**How often litigated**|Less common|**Most litigated** form|

[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## Quid Pro Quo Requirements

1. Plaintiff is a **protected class**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
2. Harassment was **unwelcome**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
3. Harassment was **based on sex**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
4. Harassment was by a **supervisor**[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    
5. There was a potential **adverse tangible employment action** (denied raise, demotion, etc.)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)
    

## Hostile Work Environment — Employer's Affirmative Defense

An employer **avoids liability** if they can show:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

1. A written policy exists for investigating and acting on sexual harassment complaints
    
2. Upon learning of (or when they _should have_ learned of) the harassment, they **promptly investigated**
    
3. They took **reasonable corrective action** (suspension, termination, reassignment of harasser)
    

## Customer Harassment of Employees

Employers **can be liable** for a customer sexually harassing an employee if the employer fails to take reasonable action. Example: A male customer repeatedly slapped a server — the manager said "it's part of the job." Court ruled the employer should have removed the customer/called police for trespass.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## Real-World Example from Lecture

A utility manager supervisor dated subordinates (against policy), got dumped, and kept harassing the employee. HR investigated → 2-week suspension → required to sign a 6-month no-harassment agreement (strategic move to have written admission of guilt). Three months later, he used a sexual slang term for "cat" toward a Halloween-costumed employee → **fired that same afternoon**.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## Supreme Court "Dumbass Women" Case

A company president called only women "dumbasses," asked only women to retrieve coins from his front pants pocket, pick objects up from the floor, and invited a female employee to the Holiday Inn to "negotiate a raise". Filed with the EEOC → sued under **hostile work environment** (not quid pro quo) → **she prevailed** in a landmark Supreme Court ruling. Court also affirmed: employees are **not entitled to a utopian work environment**, but illegal sex-based behavior is still prohibited.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/8fb659ba-84bc-4cee-be86-7a6701eb3f09/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Pt2-Video-Transcript-1.pdf)

## V. Pregnancy Discrimination Act (1978)

An **amendment to the Civil Rights Act**. Requires **15+ employees**.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## What It Covers

- Women who **are** pregnant[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Women who **could become** pregnant (does not require actual pregnancy)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Does **not** protect men directly, but indirectly protects household income[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    

## Key Case — Battery Company

A manufacturing plant excluded women of childbearing age because chemical exposure led to higher miscarriage and birth defect rates. The Supreme Court ruled this **illegal** because:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

- Men at the same plant also had elevated miscarriage/birth defect rates for their families — yet the company did **not** exclude men of childbearing age
    
- The company's real motive was limiting the female labor pool, not protecting health

## VI. Age Discrimination in Employment Act (ADEA, 1967)

Amended in 1978 and 1986. Before the ADEA, employers could force retirement at age 65.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## Key Rules

- Protects individuals **over 40** years old[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Applies to employers with **20 or more employees** (higher threshold than the 15-employee Civil Rights Act requirement)[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Covers: hiring, firing, promotion, compensation, training, and all privileges of employment[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Workers **under 40** have **no ADEA protection** — an employer can legally discriminate against a 22-year-old for being "too young or inexperienced"[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- A small percentage of high-level executive positions can still have mandatory retirement ages[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## VII. Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA)

Applies to employers with **15+ employees**.[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## Definition of Disability (Three Prongs)

To be protected, the individual must have:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

1. A **physical or mental impairment** that **substantially limits a major life activity**, AND
    
2. A **record of that impairment** (so the employer is aware), OR
    
3. The employer **regards them as having** an impairment (even if they don't actually have one)
    

## Reasonable Accommodation Standard

The employer must accommodate unless it creates **undue financial hardship**:[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

- A small for-profit college with $20,000 in annual profit cannot be forced to spend $50,000 on an elevator[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- Purdue, a large institution, **could** be required to install an elevator or relocate a disabled professor's office to the ground floor[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    

## What Is (and Isn't) a Disability

| Condition                                   | Protected Under ADA?                                                     |
| ------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Blindness, paralysis, wheelchair use        | ✅ Yes                                                                    |
| Obesity                                     | ✅ Yes                                                                    |
| Alcoholism                                  | ✅ Yes (but job-performance issues can still be grounds for termination)  |
| Dyslexia                                    | ✅ Yes                                                                    |
| Being "regarded as" disabled (even falsely) | ✅ Yes                                                                    |
| Sinusitis / sinus problems                  | ❌ No (courts ruled it doesn't substantially limit a major life activity) |

[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)

## Important ADA Nuances

- An **alcoholic employee** who performs their job well cannot be denied employment at another company simply because a reference reveals their alcoholism — that's **illegal** discrimination[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- An employer who fires someone because they _think_ the person is dyslexic (even if they aren't) has committed **illegal discrimination** under the "regarded as" prong[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **Obesity case**: An employee who left and reapplied was told to lose weight before being rehired — **illegal**, because she could still do the job[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
- **ADA protects customers** — unlike the Civil Rights Act, which does not[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
    
    - Example: An adult nightclub in LA was required to make its shower stall ADA-compliant even though no disabled employee had ever applied — ADA doesn't require a proven past victim, just the possibility of a future one[](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/118389950/914b7bde-247e-4a69-a7a5-1e40e3a07f00/Chapter-40-Employment-Law-Continued-3-Video-Transcript.pdf)
        

## ADA vs. Civil Rights Act — Key Differences

|Feature|Civil Rights Act|ADA|
|---|---|---|
|Employee threshold|15+|15+|
|Protects customers?|❌ No|✅ Yes|
|Reasonable accommodation?|Religion only|All covered disabilities|
|Legislature defines protected class?|Yes|No — courts define "disability"|
