##### Lower variability - lower waiting time #####
Reducing Variability
	Reservation
		Control when customers show up
			Have a very regular interval
	Steering Demand
		Early bird specials
		Happy Hour
		Hotel discount on off peak days
			Incentivize customers to reduce variability
Priority Rules
	Order in which we permit the customer/flow units access to the server (resource)
	Service time dependent priority rule
		Shortest Processing Time rule is the most commonly used
			next available resource is given to customer with shortest (average) processing time
		==When service time does not depend on sequence==
		![[Pasted image 20260302125234.png]]
	Service Time independent priority rule
		most often used priority rule based on arrival process is First come first serve
			Other rules
			Emergency
			Value of customer
	Resource Pooling
		2 separate checkout lanes vs 1 common lane but with 2 parallel cashiers
			having a common queue and whichever cashier is available then next person in line goes to that cashier.
				More efficient

#### Calculating x bar and r bar (sample mean and range) ####
Sampling
	Grab (n) units for a sample day 1
	do this again (m) days
Sample Mean (x̄)
	values for each unit in sample ÷ (n) number of units
Sample Range (R)
	Maximum - Minimum
